# Danielle & Co. Consulting — Meta Carousel Ad Concepts

Five carousel concepts for Meta (Facebook/Instagram) driving private-practice owners and
medical-group leaders to daniellecoconsulting.com to book a free operations assessment.

**Verified facts used (nothing else invented):**
- 12+ years in multi-site regional healthcare operations, including Forefront Dermatology
- Managed/optimized a $10M+ monthly revenue portfolio across multiple sites
- Led a paperless digital transformation that increased efficiency 43% while reducing labor demands
- Services: staffing & team management; scheduling & patient flow; workflow standardization/playbooks;
  multi-site operations & new office openings; performance & budget optimization
- No testimonials, no client counts, no certifications/HIPAA badges are claimed anywhere below.

**Brand system:** navy `#1B2A4A`, warm off-white `#F7F5F1`, gold `#C98A3B` accent, serif
(Fraunces-like) headlines, "D&Co." monogram, no stock photos of doctors, no stethoscope/cross
clichés, no AI-generated people.

---

## Concept 1 — "It Keeps Coming Back" (tried-everything pain)

**Angle rationale:** Most practice owners have already thrown money at this problem — a new
scheduling tool, a new front-desk hire, a new EHR module — and the same fires reignite within a
quarter. This concept names that exhaustion directly and reframes the issue: they've been treating
symptoms, not the underlying workflow. It works as a cold-audience scroll-stopper because it
matches an internal monologue almost verbatim ("didn't we just fix this?").

**Cards (5):**
1. **Headline:** "You fixed it. It broke again." **Support:** New software. New hire. Same 6pm scramble.
2. **Headline:** "The problem was never the tool." **Support:** It's the workflow underneath it.
3. **Headline:** "Staffing gaps. Patient backlogs. Budget surprises." **Support:** Different symptoms, same root cause.
4. **Headline:** "12+ years finding the root cause." **Support:** Multi-site healthcare operations, start to finish.
5. **Headline:** "Get the real diagnosis." **Support:** Book a free operations assessment.

**Primary text (125 char target):** "Tried a new scheduling tool. New front-desk hire. Still the same 6pm scramble? The fix was never the tool. Free ops assessment."

**Headline (40 char target):** "Same Problem, New Tool? Not Anymore."

**Description:** 12+ years fixing healthcare operations at the root cause, not the symptom.

**CTA button:** Book Now

**Targeting suggestion:** Job titles Practice Owner / Practice Manager / Office Manager /
Practice Administrator in healthcare; interests in practice-management software and small
medical-business ownership; 1% lookalike of site visitors/assessment bookers; broad geo (US),
age 32-60; exclude current clients if a CRM audience exists.

## Concept 2 — Multi-Site Inconsistency (for group leaders)

**Angle rationale:** Regional and multi-location leaders have a different pain than solo owners:
not "is this broken" but "which of my five locations is doing it right, and why don't the other
four match." This concept speaks to leaders who already sense the variance problem but haven't
named it as a solvable operations issue rather than a "culture" or "people" issue.

**Cards (5):**
1. **Headline:** "Every location runs it differently." **Support:** Which one is actually right?
2. **Headline:** "One playbook. Every site." **Support:** Standardized workflows, real consistency.
3. **Headline:** "$10M+ portfolios. One standard." **Support:** Not five sites guessing separately.
4. **Headline:** "Built for multi-site growth." **Support:** Staffing, scheduling, and performance — aligned.
5. **Headline:** "See what one standard looks like." **Support:** Book a free operations assessment.

**Primary text (125 char target):** "Five locations, five different ways of doing things? One standardized playbook fixes that — free operations assessment."

**Headline (40 char target):** "One Standard For Every Location"

**Description:** Multi-site operations consulting for medical groups managing more than one site.

**CTA button:** Get Started

**Targeting suggestion:** Job titles Regional Director of Operations / VP Operations / Multi-Site
Practice Administrator in healthcare; company size mid-market healthcare groups; interests in
healthcare franchising and group-practice management; lookalike of assessment bookers; US,
age 35-60.

## Concept 3 — New-Office-Opening Overwhelm

**Angle rationale:** A practice or group about to open a new location is at a decision-rich,
high-stakes moment — staffing, scheduling systems, and workflow all have to exist before day one,
and most owners are doing it from scratch with no playbook. This concept catches that specific,
time-boxed anxiety rather than a generic "we have ops problems" message.

**Cards (4):**
1. **Headline:** "Opening a new office?" **Support:** The checklist is longer than you think.
2. **Headline:** "Staffing. Scheduling. Systems. Day one." **Support:** Built before you open the doors, not after.
3. **Headline:** "New office openings, done before." **Support:** Multi-site operations, start to finish.
4. **Headline:** "Plan it right the first time." **Support:** Book a free operations assessment.

**Primary text (125 char target):** "New office on the calendar? Get staffing, scheduling, and workflow built before day one — free operations assessment."

**Headline (40 char target):** "Open Your New Office Right"

**Description:** New-office-opening and multi-site operations consulting for growing practices.

**CTA button:** Book Now

**Targeting suggestion:** Practice owners and group leadership in active-growth phase; interests
in medical real estate / practice acquisition / franchise development; custom audience of
recent expansion-related site visitors if pixel data supports it; US, age 32-60.

## Concept 4 — The 43% / $10M Proof Angle *(strongest concept — full image set)*

**Angle rationale:** With no testimonials and no client logos available, verifiable numbers are
the strongest trust asset this brand has. This concept leads with hard, specific proof instead of
a promise, which reads as more credible to skeptical, financially literate decision-makers
(owners, CFOs, group leadership) than a pain-agitation hook. Specificity is the differentiator —
"43%" and "$10M+" are much harder to wave off than "we'll optimize your operations."

**Cards (5):**
1. **Headline:** "43% more efficient." **Support:** One digital transformation. A real number.
2. **Headline:** "$10M+ monthly revenue portfolio." **Support:** Managed and optimized across multiple sites.
3. **Headline:** "12+ years in healthcare operations." **Support:** Regional multi-site experience, including Forefront Dermatology.
4. **Headline:** "Less labor. More output." **Support:** A paperless workflow that actually reduced the load on staff.
5. **Headline:** "See what this looks like for your practice." **Support:** Book a free operations assessment.

**Primary text (125 char target):** "A paperless workflow overhaul lifted efficiency 43% inside a $10M+ monthly revenue portfolio. Free operations assessment."

**Headline (40 char target):** "43% More Efficient. Proven At Scale."

**Description:** 12+ years of multi-site healthcare operations results — not theory.

**CTA button:** Learn More

**Targeting suggestion:** Practice owners, CFOs, and healthcare group leadership; interests in
healthcare business management and operations efficiency; retargeting of website visitors who
did not book; lookalike of past assessment bookers; US, age 35-65.

## Concept 5 — "Advice vs. Implementation" Reframe

**Angle rationale:** Many practice owners have already paid for a consultant who delivered a
slide deck and left. This concept differentiates Danielle & Co. by pointing at that exact failure
mode — advice without execution — and positioning 12+ years of actually running operations
(not just recommending changes) as the real gap being filled. It resonates with owners who are
consultant-fatigued rather than consultant-naive.

**Cards (4):**
1. **Headline:** "Another consultant. Another slide deck." **Support:** Then nothing changes.
2. **Headline:** "Advice isn't the gap. Execution is." **Support:** Someone has to actually run it.
3. **Headline:** "12+ years running operations, not just advising on them." **Support:** Staffing, scheduling, and budgets — implemented.
4. **Headline:** "Get a plan you can actually run." **Support:** Book a free operations assessment.

**Primary text (125 char target):** "Another slide deck won't fix your schedule. Get someone who's actually run multi-site healthcare ops. Free assessment."

**Headline (40 char target):** "Advice Is Cheap. Execution Isn't."

**Description:** Hands-on healthcare operations consulting — implementation, not just recommendations.

**CTA button:** Book Now

**Targeting suggestion:** Practice owners who have previously engaged consultants (interests:
management consulting, business coaching, small-business advisory); lookalike of assessment
bookers; retargeting of blog/resource-page visitors; US, age 35-60.
